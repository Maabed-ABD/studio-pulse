The case for asking

Why a questionnaire works
when morale is low

A well-designed questionnaire does three things at once — it listens, it diagnoses, and it co-creates. That's what makes it the right tool for this moment.

01
🧭
Gives stress a shape
People leave surveys feeling heard. Naming what's wrong is the first step to fixing it — and it shifts energy from passive frustration to active input.
→ Morale
02
🤝
Rebuilds shared vision
After a hard stretch, people drift. A questionnaire resurfaces what everyone values — career growth, collaboration, and the work they actually want to do.
→ Alignment
03
📐
Creates a brief for change
The results become your roadmap. You can't redesign a studio culture without a brief — this is how you write one, built from the inside out.
→ Strategy
#1
The golden rule: Only run this if leadership is genuinely ready to act on the results. A questionnaire that leads to silence is worse than none at all — it confirms the team's fear that their voice doesn't matter.
The framework — 3 phases
Phase 01
🌡️
Emotional baseline
Feel
Open with mood and energy questions. Simple scales, not essays. This reveals the real state of the team before asking about work or direction.
Mood 1–5 Energy level Belonging
Phase 02
⚙️
Work & collaboration
Think
Move into work: communication, workload, collaboration, career path. Use multiple choice + one open field per section. Max 12 questions total.
Team dynamics Workload Career clarity
Phase 03
🌱
Vision & aspiration
Dream
End with aspiration. Ask what excites them, what projects they want, and what one change would make the biggest difference. Vision gets co-created here.
Dream projects Culture wish One bold idea
